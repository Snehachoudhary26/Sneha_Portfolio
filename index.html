About

Who I am and how I got here.

Sneha Choudhary · Jabalpur, India

I didn't grow up dreaming in code. I grew up curious — about how things work, why systems break, and what it takes to fix them. That curiosity is what eventually pulled me into computer science.

I'm a final-year Computer Science student at RGPV Bhopal. I started with the fundamentals — data structures, algorithms, web development — and kept asking "what if I built this myself?" That question turned into 500+ LeetCode problems solved, five shipped projects, and a growing comfort with everything from React frontends to Flutter mobile apps to Gemini-powered AI features.

Somewhere along the way, AI stopped being a buzzword and became a tool I actually use — to build smarter chatbots, predict prices with machine learning, and make apps that genuinely help people. I've interned in Java development, data science, and even spent time learning Linux and cybersecurity fundamentals, because I'd rather understand a system end-to-end than just one layer of it.

I'm currently looking for opportunities where I can keep building things that matter — and keep learning faster than I thought possible.

What I Believe

The ideas that shape how I build and learn.

01

Understanding beats memorizing.

Solving 500+ DSA problems taught me less about syntax and more about how to think through a problem before writing a single line of code.

02

Ship it, then improve it.

A live project with rough edges teaches you more than a perfect one that never leaves your laptop. Every one of my projects went live before it felt "ready."

03

AI is a tool, not a shortcut.

I use Gemini and machine learning to solve real problems — price prediction, spam detection, recommendations — not to skip understanding how things work underneath.

04

Depth matters as much as breadth.

Learning Linux and Kali Linux fundamentals wasn't required for my degree. I did it anyway, because understanding the system below your code makes you a better builder.
